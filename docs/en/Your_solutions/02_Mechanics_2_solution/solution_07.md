To solve this, we need to analyze the forces acting on the **10 kg block** (the bottom block). Since the 5 kg block (top) is tied to the wall, it cannot move, but it will exert a frictional force on the 10 kg block as it slides underneath.

---

### 1. Identify the Masses and Normal Forces
* **$m_1$ (Top block):** $5 \text{ kg}$
* **$m_2$ (Bottom block):** $10 \text{ kg}$
* **$g$ (Gravity):** $9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$
* **$\mu_k$ (Kinetic friction):** $0.2$

There are two surfaces where friction occurs:
1.  **Surface A (between the blocks):** The normal force here is just the weight of the top block ($N_1 = m_1g$).
2.  **Surface B (between bottom block and floor):** The normal force here is the total weight of both blocks ($N_2 = (m_1 + m_2)g$).



---

### 2. Calculate Frictional Forces ($f_k$)
The 10 kg block has to overcome friction at both the top and the bottom surfaces.

* **Friction from Top Block ($f_{k1}$):**
    $$f_{k1} = \mu_k \times (m_1g) = 0.2 \times (5 \times 9.8) = 9.8 \text{ N}$$
* **Friction from Floor ($f_{k2}$):**
    $$f_{k2} = \mu_k \times (m_1 + m_2)g = 0.2 \times (15 \times 9.8) = 29.4 \text{ N}$$

---

### 3. Calculate Net Force and Acceleration
Now we look at the horizontal forces acting on the **10 kg block**:
* **Applied Force ($F_{app}$):** $45 \text{ N}$ (forward)
* **Friction from Top ($f_{k1}$):** $9.8 \text{ N}$ (backward)
* **Friction from Floor ($f_{k2}$):** $29.4 \text{ N}$ (backward)

**Net Force Equation:**
$$F_{net} = F_{app} - f_{k1} - f_{k2}$$
$$F_{net} = 45 - 9.8 - 29.4 = 5.8 \text{ N}$$

**Acceleration ($a$):**
Using Newton's Second Law ($F = ma$) for the 10 kg block:
$$a = \frac{F_{net}}{m_2}$$
$$a = \frac{5.8}{10} = 0.58 \text{ m/s}^2$$

**The acceleration of the 10 kg block is $0.58 \text{ m/s}^2$.**
