To solve for the spring constant and total energy, we compare the given displacement equation to the standard form for **Simple Harmonic Motion (SHM)**:

$$x(t) = A \cos(\omega t)$$

From your equation $x(t) = 0.2 \cos(10\pi t)$, we can identify:
* **Amplitude ($A$):** $0.2 \text{ m}$
* **Angular frequency ($\omega$):** $10\pi \text{ rad/s}$
* **Mass ($m$):** $10 \text{ kg}$

---

### 1. Finding the Spring Constant ($k$)
The relationship between angular frequency, mass, and the spring constant is given by:
$$\omega = \sqrt{\frac{k}{m}} \implies \omega^2 = \frac{k}{m}$$



**Step-by-Step Calculation:**
1.  **Rearrange for $k$:** $$k = m \omega^2$$
2.  **Substitute the values:**
    $$k = 10 \times (10\pi)^2$$
    $$k = 10 \times 100\pi^2$$
3.  **Final Result:**
    $$k = 1000\pi^2 \approx 9869.6 \text{ N/m}$$

---

### 2. Total Mechanical Energy ($E_{total}$)
In an ideal mass-spring system, the total energy is conserved and is equal to the maximum potential energy stored in the spring when it is at its maximum displacement (amplitude).

The formula for total energy is:
$$E = \frac{1}{2} k A^2$$



**Step-by-Step Calculation:**
1.  **Substitute the values ($k = 1000\pi^2$ and $A = 0.2$):**
    $$E = \frac{1}{2} (1000\pi^2) (0.2)^2$$
2.  **Calculate the square of the amplitude:**
    $$0.2^2 = 0.04$$
3.  **Solve:**
    $$E = 500\pi^2 \times 0.04$$
    $$E = 20\pi^2 \text{ J}$$
4.  **Numerical approximation (using $\pi^2 \approx 9.87$):**
    $$E \approx 197.4 \text{ J}$$

**Summary:**
* **Spring Constant ($k$):** $1000\pi^2 \text{ N/m}$
* **Total Energy ($E$):** $20\pi^2 \text{ J}$
