To find the final speed of the combined mass, we need to break this problem into two distinct phases: the **descent** (using Energy) and the **collision** (using Momentum).

---

### Phase 1: The Descent (Conservation of Energy)
As the 0.5 kg block ($m_1$) slides down the frictionless track, its gravitational potential energy is converted into kinetic energy.

**Formula:**
$$m_1 g h = \frac{1}{2} m_1 v_1^2$$



**Calculation:**
* **$m_1$:** 0.5 kg
* **$g$:** $9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$
* **$h$:** 3.0 m

Solving for velocity at the bottom ($v_1$):
$$v_1 = \sqrt{2gh} = \sqrt{2 \times 9.8 \times 3.0} = \sqrt{58.8}$$
$$v_1 \approx 7.67 \text{ m/s}$$

---

### Phase 2: The Collision (Conservation of Momentum)
At the bottom, the 0.5 kg block hits the 1.5 kg block ($m_2$). Because they stick together, this is a **perfectly inelastic collision**.

**Formula:**
$$m_1 v_1 + m_2 v_2 = (m_1 + m_2) v_f$$





**Calculation:**
* **Initial momentum:** $(0.5 \text{ kg} \times 7.67 \text{ m/s}) + (1.5 \text{ kg} \times 0 \text{ m/s}) = 3.835 \text{ kg}\cdot\text{m/s}$
* **Total mass ($m_1 + m_2$):** $0.5 \text{ kg} + 1.5 \text{ kg} = 2.0 \text{ kg}$

**Solve for $v_f$:**
$$3.835 = 2.0 \times v_f$$
$$v_f = \frac{3.835}{2.0}$$
$$v_f \approx 1.92 \text{ m/s}$$

**The speed of the combined mass just after the collision is approximately 1.92 m/s.**
