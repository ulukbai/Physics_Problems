To find the speed at the bottom of the swing, we use the **Law of Conservation of Energy**. The gravitational potential energy ($PE$) at the release height is converted entirely into kinetic energy ($KE$) at the lowest point.

---

### 1. Finding the Vertical Height ($h$)
First, we need to determine how high the bob is lifted relative to its lowest point. When the pendulum is at an angle $\theta$, its vertical position is $L \cos\theta$ from the pivot. The height $h$ above the bottom is:
$$h = L - L \cos\theta = L(1 - \cos\theta)$$



**Given Values:**
* **Length ($L$):** $1.0 \text{ m}$
* **Angle ($\theta$):** $15^\circ$
* **Gravity ($g$):** $9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$

**Calculation:**
$$h = 1.0 \times (1 - \cos 15^\circ)$$
$$h \approx 1.0 \times (1 - 0.9659)$$
$$h \approx 0.0341 \text{ m}$$

---

### 2. Calculating the Speed ($v$)
At the bottom of the swing, all potential energy has turned into kinetic energy:
$$mgh = \frac{1}{2}mv^2$$

Notice that the mass ($m$) cancels out on both sides, meaning the speed depends only on the height and gravity:
$$gh = \frac{1}{2}v^2 \implies v = \sqrt{2gh}$$





**Calculation:**
1.  **Plug in the values:**
    $$v = \sqrt{2 \times 9.8 \times 0.0341}$$
2.  **Solve:**
    $$v = \sqrt{0.66836}$$
    $$v \approx 0.817 \text{ m/s}$$

**The speed of the pendulum bob at the bottom of its swing is approximately 0.82 m/s.**
