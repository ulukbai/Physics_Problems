To determine which object has greater momentum, we use the formula for linear momentum:

$$p = m \cdot v$$

where $m$ is the mass and $v$ is the velocity. To compare them accurately, we should ensure the units are consistent.

### 1. Momentum of the Fly
* **Mass ($m_1$):** $2 \text{ g} = 0.002 \text{ kg}$
* **Velocity ($v_1$):** $10 \text{ m/s}$

$$p_{\text{fly}} = 0.002 \text{ kg} \cdot 10 \text{ m/s} = 0.02 \text{ kg}\cdot\text{m/s}$$

---

### 2. Momentum of the Tennis Ball
* **Mass ($m_2$):** $60 \text{ g} = 0.06 \text{ kg}$
* **Velocity ($v_2$):** $1 \text{ m/s}$

$$p_{\text{ball}} = 0.06 \text{ kg} \cdot 1 \text{ m/s} = 0.06 \text{ kg}\cdot\text{m/s}$$

---

### 3. Comparison
Comparing the two values:
* $p_{\text{fly}} = 0.02 \text{ kg}\cdot\text{m/s}$
* $p_{\text{ball}} = 0.06 \text{ kg}\cdot\text{m/s}$



**Result:** The **60-gram tennis ball** has greater momentum. Even though the fly is moving ten times faster, the tennis ball's mass is thirty times greater, resulting in a momentum that is three times larger than that of the fly.
