To solve this, we need to look at the boat's velocity relative to the water so that its **resultant velocity** points exactly North.

### 1. Finding the Heading (Angle)

Think of this as a right-triangle problem. To cancel out the river's eastward flow, the boat must aim slightly **West of North**.

*   **Velocity of the River ($v_r$):** $2 \text{ m/s}$ [East]
*   **Velocity of the Boat in still water ($v_b$):** $5 \text{ m/s}$ [Heading angle]
*   **Desired Resultant Velocity ($v_{res}$):** [North]



We can find the angle $\theta$ (measured West of North) using trigonometry:

$$\sin(\theta) = \frac{\text{v}_{river}}{\text{v}_{boat}}$$
$$\sin(\theta) = \frac{2}{5} = 0.4$$
$$\theta = \arcsin(0.4) \approx 23.58^\circ$$

**The boat should head $23.58^\circ$ West of North.**

---

### 2. Finding the Time to Cross

To find the time, we first need the boat's **actual speed** moving North. This is the vertical component of our vector triangle, which we find using the Pythagorean theorem:

$$v_{res} = \sqrt{v_b^2 - v_r^2}$$
$$v_{res} = \sqrt{5^2 - 2^2} = \sqrt{25 - 4} = \sqrt{21} \approx 4.58 \text{ m/s}$$

Now, use the standard formula for time:

$$\text{Time} = \frac{\text{Distance}}{\text{Speed (North)}}$$
$$\text{Time} = \frac{200 \text{ m}}{4.58 \text{ m/s}} \approx 43.67 \text{ seconds}$$
