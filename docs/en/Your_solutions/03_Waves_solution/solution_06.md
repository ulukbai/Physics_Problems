To solve this, we compare the given wave equation to the standard form of a traveling wave equation:

$$y(x,t) = A \sin(kx - \omega t)$$

From your equation $y(x,t) = 0.05 \sin(2\pi x - 50\pi t)$, we can identify the following parameters:
* **Amplitude ($A$):** $0.05$
* **Wave number ($k$):** $2\pi$
* **Angular frequency ($\omega$):** $50\pi$

---

### a) Amplitude ($A$)
The amplitude is the maximum displacement from the equilibrium position, which is the coefficient in front of the sine function.
**$A = 0.05 \text{ m}$**

### b) Wavelength ($\lambda$)
The wave number $k$ is related to the wavelength by the formula $k = \frac{2\pi}{\lambda}$.
$$2\pi = \frac{2\pi}{\lambda}$$
$$\lambda = \frac{2\pi}{2\pi}$$
**$\lambda = 1 \text{ m}$**

### c) Frequency ($f$)
The angular frequency $\omega$ is related to the frequency by the formula $\omega = 2\pi f$.
$$50\pi = 2\pi f$$
$$f = \frac{50\pi}{2\pi}$$
**$f = 25 \text{ Hz}$**

### d) Wave speed ($v$)
The wave speed can be calculated using the relationship between wavelength and frequency ($v = f\lambda$) or by using the ratio of angular frequency to wave number ($v = \frac{\omega}{k}$).

Using $v = f\lambda$:
$$v = 25 \text{ Hz} \times 1 \text{ m}$$
**$v = 25 \text{ m/s}$**

---

> **Quick Tip:** In wave equations of the form $\sin(kx - \omega t)$, the wave is traveling in the **positive x-direction**. If the sign between the terms were a plus ($+$), the wave would be traveling in the negative x-direction!
