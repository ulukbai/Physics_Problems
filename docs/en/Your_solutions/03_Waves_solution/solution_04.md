To find the phase difference between two points on a wave, we use the relationship between the physical distance (path difference) and the angular displacement (phase).

### 1. The Relationship Formula
A full wavelength ($\lambda$) corresponds to a full cycle of $2\pi$ radians (or $360^{\circ}$). The phase difference ($\Delta \phi$) for any distance ($\Delta x$) is given by:

$$\Delta \phi = \frac{2\pi}{\lambda} \cdot \Delta x$$



### 2. Calculation
Given that the distance between the two points is $\Delta x = \frac{\lambda}{3}$:

$$\Delta \phi = \frac{2\pi}{\lambda} \cdot \left( \frac{\lambda}{3} \right)$$

The $\lambda$ terms cancel out:

$$\Delta \phi = \frac{2\pi}{3} \text{ radians}$$

### 3. Conversion to Degrees (Optional)
To help visualize this, you can convert the radians to degrees:
$$\frac{2\pi}{3} \text{ rad} \times \left( \frac{180^{\circ}}{\pi} \right) = 120^{\circ}$$

### Summary
The phase difference between the two points is **$\frac{2\pi}{3}$ radians** (or $120^{\circ}$).
