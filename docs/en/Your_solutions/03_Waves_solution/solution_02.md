To find the speed of the wave on the guitar string, we can use the relationship between the string length, the wavelength of the fundamental frequency, and the wave speed.

### 1. Identify the Wavelength ($\lambda$)
For a string fixed at both ends, the fundamental frequency (the first harmonic) occurs when the length of the string ($L$) corresponds to exactly half of a wavelength. This creates a single "loop" or antinode in the center.

The formula relating length and wavelength for the fundamental frequency is:
$$L = \frac{\lambda}{2} \implies \lambda = 2L$$

Given:
* Length ($L$) = $64 \text{ cm} = 0.64 \text{ m}$

$$\lambda = 2 \times 0.64 \text{ m} = 1.28 \text{ m}$$



### 2. Calculate the Wave Speed ($v$)
The wave speed is the product of the frequency and the wavelength:
$$v = f \cdot \lambda$$

Given:
* Fundamental frequency ($f$) = $330 \text{ Hz}$
* Wavelength ($\lambda$) = $1.28 \text{ m}$

$$v = 330 \text{ Hz} \times 1.28 \text{ m} = 422.4 \text{ m/s}$$

### Summary
The speed of the wave on this guitar string is **$422.4 \text{ m/s}$**.
