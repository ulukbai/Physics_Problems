To find the distance to the cliff, we use the principle of echo ranging. An echo is the reflection of sound waves off a surface. In this scenario, the sound travels from the person to the cliff and then back to the person.

### 1. Identify the Given Information
* **Time for the echo ($t$):** $1 \text{ s}$
* **Speed of sound ($v$):** $343 \text{ m/s}$

### 2. Understand the Path of Sound
The total time of $1 \text{ s}$ represents the time it takes for the sound to travel the distance to the cliff **and** back again. Therefore, the sound travels the distance ($d$) twice.



### 3. The Formula
The formula for distance is:
$$\text{Distance} = \text{Speed} \times \text{Time}$$

Since we want to find the distance to the cliff ($d$), and the total distance traveled is $2d$, the formula becomes:
$$2d = v \times t$$
$$d = \frac{v \times t}{2}$$

### 4. Calculation
Substitute the known values into the equation:
$$d = \frac{343 \text{ m/s} \times 1 \text{ s}}{2}$$
$$d = \frac{343 \text{ m}}{2}$$
$$d = 171.5 \text{ m}$$

**Final Answer:**
The cliff is **$171.5 \text{ meters}$** away.
