The System of EquationsEq A: $2x + 3y = 12$Eq B: $x - y = 1$Step 1: Algebraic Solution (Substitution)Substitution is often the most intuitive method for a presentation because it follows a logical "replacement" story.Rearrange Eq B: Solve for $x$ to make it easy to plug into the other equation.$$x = y + 1$$Substitute into Eq A: Replace the $x$ in the first equation with $(y + 1)$.$$2(y + 1) + 3y = 12$$Distribute and Combine:$$2y + 2 + 3y = 12$$$$5y + 2 = 12$$Solve for $y$:$$5y = 10$$$y = 2$Find $x$: Plug $y = 2$ back into our rearranged Eq B.$$x = 2 + 1$$$x = 3$Step 2: Geometric InterpretationIn a presentation, you should explain that these equations represent two straight lines on a graph. The solution to the system is the point where these two lines cross.Line 1 ($2x + 3y = 12$): This line passes through $(6, 0)$ and $(0, 4)$.Line 2 ($x - y = 1$): This line passes through $(1, 0)$ and $(0, -1)$.The Intersection: Both lines meet exactly at the coordinates $(3, 2)$.Final SolutionThe values that satisfy both equations are:$x = 3, y = 2$
