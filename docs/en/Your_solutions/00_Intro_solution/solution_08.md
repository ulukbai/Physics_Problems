To calculate the area under the curve of the function $f(x) = \sin(x)$ from $x=0$ to $x=\pi$, we use the definite integral:

$$\text{Area} = \int_{0}^{\pi} \sin(x) \, dx$$

### 1. Find the Antiderivative
The antiderivative of $\sin(x)$ is $-\cos(x)$. According to the Fundamental Theorem of Calculus:
$$\int_{a}^{b} f(x) \, dx = F(b) - F(a)$$

### 2. Evaluate the Integral
We substitute the upper and lower limits into the antiderivative:
$$\text{Area} = \left[ -\cos(x) \right]_{0}^{\pi}$$
$$\text{Area} = (-\cos(\pi)) - (-\cos(0))$$

### 3. Substitute Trigonometric Values
Recall the values of the cosine function at these points:
* $\cos(\pi) = -1$
* $\cos(0) = 1$

Now, plug them into the equation:
$$\text{Area} = (-(-1)) - (-(1))$$
$$\text{Area} = 1 + 1$$
$$\text{Area} = 2$$



### Summary
The total area under one hump of the sine curve (from $0$ to $\pi$) is exactly **2 square units**.
