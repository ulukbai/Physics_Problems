To find the components of a vector, we use the trigonometric relationships between the magnitude of the vector (the hypotenuse) and its projections onto the coordinate axes.

Given:
* **Magnitude ($|\vec{A}|$):** $15$
* **Angle ($\theta$):** $60^\circ$

### 1. Horizontal Component ($A_x$)
The horizontal component is the side adjacent to the angle $\theta$. We use the cosine function:
$$A_x = |\vec{A}| \cos(\theta)$$
$$A_x = 15 \cdot \cos(60^\circ)$$
Since $\cos(60^\circ) = 0.5$:
$$A_x = 15 \cdot 0.5 = 7.5$$

### 2. Vertical Component ($A_y$)
The vertical component is the side opposite the angle $\theta$. We use the sine function:
$$A_y = |\vec{A}| \sin(\theta)$$
$$A_y = 15 \cdot \sin(60^\circ)$$
Since $\sin(60^\circ) = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \approx 0.866$:
$$A_y = 15 \cdot 0.866 \approx 12.99$$
